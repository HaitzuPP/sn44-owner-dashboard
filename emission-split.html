01 · Investor structure
Yuma/DCG holds an allocation of SN44 owner emissions
Yuma, a Digital Currency Group company, appears as a dedicated hotkey receiving ~11.3% of gross SN44 alpha liquidations. The on-chain proof: May 23 2025, Owner44 unstaked exactly 1,327.93 τ while Yuma staked exactly 1,327.93 τ on the same day. Matched unstake/restake = intentional investor allocation. This is not a validator fee. It's an equity-style split of subnet owner income.
1,556 τ · ~$554K · Yuma/DCG gross proceeds
02 · Transfer mechanism
Alpha can't be sent — it requires an unstake/restake pair
On Bittensor, alpha is bound to a hotkey — you can't transfer it like a token. To move an allocation to an investor, the subnet owner unstakes from their hotkey (alpha → TAO) and immediately restakes to the investor's hotkey (TAO → alpha). The May 23 matched pair is the on-chain fingerprint of this operation. Yuma's position then grew further via emissions before they liquidated the full position in August at $354–$356 / TAO.
May 23 2025 · 1,327.93 τ each side · same block window
03 · Timing & coordination
Yuma waited 3 months before selling — Owner44 moved first
Owner44 began liquidating May 22 2025. Yuma/DCG waited until Aug 20–27 2025 — a 3-month lag. This could reflect a lockup period, a coordinated drip strategy, or simply a different price target. Yuma's Aug exit price ($354–$356) was slightly below the May prices ($426) that Owner44 sold into. Yuma's 1,556 τ gross exit was also larger than the 1,327.93 τ originally transferred in, implying further alpha emissions accrued between May and August.
